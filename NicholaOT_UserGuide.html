1 · Starting up & getting around

How to open the dashboard, unlock it, and find your way around. Read this first.

What the dashboard is

The dashboard is your entire practice in one private window: clients, assessments, reports, sessions, invoices, documents and more. It runs in your web browser at app.npot.co.uk. Everything you type is saved automatically and encrypted, so only you can read it.

Opening it and unlocking

  1. Go to app.npot.co.uk in your browser (Chrome, Safari or Edge are all fine). It's worth bookmarking it.
  2. The first time ever, you'll be asked to create a master password. Choose something only you know — this password is what encrypts your data. Write it down somewhere safe; nobody, not even support, can recover it for you.
  3. Every time after that, you'll see an unlock screen. Type your master password and press Enter to open your dashboard.
Your master password cannot be reset. If you forget it, your data can only be recovered with a recovery key (we set this up in Chapter 2). Please do that step — it's your safety net.

The layout

There are three parts to every screen:

  • The sidebar (left) is your main menu — Home, Clients, Calendar, Sessions, Comms, Intake, Documents, Finances, Focus, CPD, Supervision, Library, Templates and User Guide. On a computer it's always there; on a phone, tap the menu button (top-left) to slide it out.
  • The top bar (green) holds Search everything, the notifications 🔔 bell, display options, and a cloud-sync status. When a Focus timer is running, a ⏱ pill appears here too.
  • The main area is where you actually work — it changes depending on which sidebar item you've chosen.

Two things that are true everywhere

  • Everything saves itself. There are no Save buttons. As you type, a small Saving… / Saved note appears in the top bar. You can close the tab any time and your work is there when you come back.
  • Search jumps you anywhere. Click Search everything (or press ⌘K on a Mac / Ctrl K on Windows) and start typing a client name, an invoice number or a document — it takes you straight there.
Test version vs your real data: if you ever see an orange banner at the top saying "TEST VERSION", you're in a safe practice copy with made-up sample clients — perfect for trying things out. Your real, private records are in the normal (no-banner) version.

2 · First-time setup

Do these once and the whole dashboard works smoothly. Home shows a “Set up your practice” checklist that walks you through the same steps — tick each off as you go.

Step 1 — Save a recovery key (most important)

Your recovery key is the only way back in if you forget your master password. Set it up now.

  1. Click your profile (top-right), then Settings → Security. (Or click Set up recovery key on the yellow banner if it's showing.)
  2. Choose Generate recovery key. A long code appears.
  3. Save it somewhere safe and separate from your password — print it, or store it in a password manager. Treat it like a spare front-door key.
Without a recovery key, a forgotten master password means your data is gone for good. This one step is your insurance — please don't skip it.

Step 2 — Your profile & practice details

This is the information that prints on your reports, letters and invoices.

  1. Open Settings → Personal and fill in your full name, professional credentials, HCPC number and a short bio / CV (the bio appears as the author section on Marie reports).
  2. Open Settings → Practice and add your practice name, email, phone and upload your logo if you have one. The logo appears top-left on every document.

Step 3 — Bank details, services & prices

This makes invoicing almost automatic.

  1. Open Settings → Bank Details and enter the account name, sort code and account number that should appear on invoices.
  2. In the same place, build your Services & prices list. Each entry is either per hour or a flat fee, with a price — for example “Therapy £60/hr”, “Travel £25/hr”, “Mileage 45p/mile”, “Motor co-ordination assessment £480”. These fill in automatically on invoices later (and you can still change them per invoice).

Step 4 — Turn on Cloud Sync (your off-device backup)

Cloud sync keeps an encrypted copy of your data in the cloud and a history you can roll back to.

  1. Open Settings → Cloud Sync. The web address and key are usually pre-filled.
  2. Click Connect & Test. You're looking for a green “Cloud synced” confirmation. From then on it syncs quietly in the background.
Your data is encrypted before it's uploaded — the cloud only ever holds a scrambled copy that only your password can open.

Step 5 — Set up secure intake (so online forms work)

Do this once so parents and clients can send you completed forms.

  1. Open the Intake tab (or the checklist step on Home).
  2. Click Set up secure intake. That's it — your forms can now receive submissions. (Skip this and people filling in your form will see a “not set up to receive submissions” message.)

Step 6 — Add the AI key (optional, but powers the ✨ assistant)

The sparkle writing assistant and Annie need an Anthropic API key, stored only on your device.

  1. Open Annie (the floating ✨ button, bottom-right) and follow the prompt to paste your key, or add it under Settings.
  2. One key powers everything — Annie's chat and all the ✨ Draft/Refine buttons.
When all six are done, the “Set up your practice” card on Home tidies itself away. There's also a cheeky “Celebrate your new app” tick to finish on — go on, you've earned it.

3 · The Home dashboard

Your landing page — a glance tells you what needs doing today.

Home pulls the important things to the front so nothing gets lost. From top to bottom you'll typically see:

  • Today's sessions — anyone you're seeing today, with a quick way into their note.
  • Quick-stat tiles — Active Clients, Sessions This Week, Reports Pending and Unpaid Invoices. Each tile is clickable and takes you to that list.
  • Reminders & to-dos — a card gathering follow-ups that are due, upcoming assessment/EHCP deadlines, and anything you've flagged. Overdue items are highlighted.
  • “Set up your practice” checklist — only on a fresh setup (see Chapter 2). It hides once you've finished or dismissed it.

How to use it day to day

  1. Open the dashboard each morning on Home — scan today's sessions and the reminders card.
  2. Click a tile (e.g. “3 Unpaid Invoices”) to jump straight to what needs action.
  3. Clear a follow-up by opening it, doing the task, and marking it done — it drops off the list.
The notifications 🔔 bell in the top bar mirrors the same reminders, so you'll spot overdue follow-ups from any screen, not just Home.

4 · Clients & the client record

Your caseload, and a complete record for each person in one place.

The client list

Open Clients from the sidebar to see everyone. There's a Waitlist tab for referrals you've received but not yet started. You can search and filter the list, and each row shows a status (e.g. Active, Being assessed, Discharged).

How to add a new client

  1. In Clients, click + New client (or Add to waitlist for a referral you're not starting yet).
  2. Fill in their details — name, date of birth, contact, referral information. Only the name is essential to start; you can add the rest later.
  3. Save. The new client opens straight into their record.
📄 Import an existing report: if you already have a report (PDF or a photo of one), click Import on the client list. The AI reads it and pre-fills a new client record for you to check and save — a big time-saver when taking on someone already assessed elsewhere.

Inside a client's record

5 · Running an assessment

The Assessment tab is the heart of the dashboard — every score and every line of the report live here together, so nothing is ever typed twice.

How an assessment fits together

Open a client's Assessment tab. It opens on an Introduction section, then flows in the exact order the report prints: Introduction → Background → the test sections → Summary → Outcomes → Provision → Previous Results → Resources Appendix. Use the section list on the left to jump between them.

Step 1 — Choose which tests this child had

  1. At the top you'll see “Assessments included” pills. Tap a pill to switch a test on or off for this child.
  2. Add as many as apply — MABC-2, BOT-2, VMI, SPM-2, COPM, Sensory Profile, the 🧸 Observation (pre-filled from your Observation Form template), and a free-form Ad-hoc section. Several can be active at once.
  3. Only the tests you've switched on will appear in the report and the score summary — nothing empty ever prints.

Step 2 — Enter the scores

  1. Go to a test's section. Type the raw/standard scores into its score grid.
  2. The grid auto-interprets — colouring each result red, amber or green and showing the band — so you can see the picture at a glance. Its pre-written intro text appears inline above it.

Step 3 — Write the clinical narrative

  1. Beside (or below) each score grid is a narrative box — your clinical interpretation in words.
  2. Type it yourself, drop in ready-made wording from the 💬 Phrases button, or tap the ✨ sparkle to draft it for you (Chapter 6).
  3. Repeat for Background (Reason for Referral, Developmental & Medical History, Observations), Summary (Clinical Interpretation, Functional Impact, Recommendations & Goals, Summary of OT Needs), and the rest.

Step 4 — Decide what goes in the report

Every single field has an “In report / Not needed” toggle. Switch a field to Not needed and it's excluded from this child's report (and the contents page renumbers itself). It only affects this child — your templates are untouched.

Standard vs Marie (EHCP) reports

A child can be on the standard format or the Marie / EHCP format. Marie adds six extra sections — Postural Management & Equipment, Sleep, Communication, Cognition, Mood & Behaviour and Social & Relationships — each with its own toggle and its own phrase set. They appear only on Marie reports, and only when they contain text. There's a button in the Assessment tab to switch a child between the two formats; your text is kept either way.

More than one assessment over time

A child can hold several dated assessments (an Initial, then an Annual Review, and so on). The Overview lists them, each with its own export. Choosing ➕ New assessment safely files the current one away and starts a fresh blank one — nothing is ever overwritten.

Nothing to save. The Assessment tab autosaves constantly — you'll see Saving / Saved in the top bar. There are no Save buttons anywhere.

6 · The AI writing assistant

The little ✨ sparkle drafts and polishes your clinical writing in seconds — in your voice, and it never invents scores.

How to draft a section

  1. Put a few notes in the box first if you can — the more you give it, the more it has to work with. (It can also draft from the scores and other sections you've filled in.)
  2. Tap the ✨ for an instant draft in the Balanced style.
  3. Prefer a different tone? Tap the ▾ next to it and choose a style, or Refine to tidy what's already there.
  4. Read it, edit anything you'd word differently, and move on. It's a first draft for you to own — not the final word.

The four choices on the ▾ menu

  • Plain — warm and jargon-light, the right tone for parents and carers.
  • Balanced — professional but readable. The default, and what the ✨ uses on a single tap.
  • Clinical — formal, for EHCP panels and consultants.
  • Refine — keeps your meaning but tidies the wording already in the box (greyed out when the box is empty).

If you don't like a draft — Undo

Straight after any draft or refine, a small ↶ undo button appears just to the left of the sparkle for about 30 seconds. Tap it and the box goes back exactly as it was. One click, fully reversed.

Where you'll find the ✨

It's on practically every writing box: every assessment report section, the Marie sections, the Observation and ad-hoc sections, Fine Motor, Sensory, the VMI / SPM-2 / COPM narratives, the Summary and Background fields, every session-note field (including Additional Notes), the Early Years Observation form, and the Refine fields in Templates.

Always your clinical judgement. The assistant drafts from what you've entered — it won't invent scores or facts. Read every draft before it goes into a report.
The “Read a document with AI” tools (on assessments and notes) are separate buttons, not the sparkle — they read an uploaded document for you rather than writing a section.

7 · Writing & exporting reports

Everything you typed in the Assessment tab becomes a finished, professional report — and you never re-type a word. One engine builds it, so what you see on screen, in the PDF, and in the Word file are always identical.

How the report is built

There is no separate “report document” to write. The dashboard takes the sections you filled in on the Assessment tab (Chapter 5) and lays them out for you, in order, with a title page, your logo, a contents page, the test results, your narrative, the summary, the recommendations and the resources appendix. Because a single engine produces the on-screen Preview, the PDF and the Word file, you never have to wonder whether the export “came out right” — all three always match, down to the page.

The auto-numbered Contents page

The report opens with a Contents page that numbers each section for you. You never type those numbers. If you switch a section off, the Contents page renumbers itself automatically so there are never any gaps — section 4 always follows section 3, even if you turned off what used to be section 4.

The two things that decide what prints

  • The “In report / Not needed” toggles. Every section and field has one (you met them in Chapter 5). Anything set to Not needed is left out completely, and the Contents page renumbers.
  • Empty fields simply don't print. This is the important one: any field or sub-heading you leave blank is automatically left out of the report. You don't have to tidy up after yourself. If you write the “Fine motor” part of an Observation but leave “Gross motor” blank, the finished report shows only the Fine motor heading — the empty Gross motor sub-heading never appears. A half-filled section only ever shows the parts you actually wrote.
This means you can work at your own pace and export at any time. The report only ever contains finished writing — there are no empty headings, “TBC” gaps or placeholder lines to embarrass you in front of a parent or panel.

The Assessment Process list

The report's “Assessment Process” section lists only the tests you actually carried out — the ones you switched on as pills in the Assessment tab. A test you didn't use is never mentioned, so the methods section is always an honest record of what happened.

The Resources Appendix

If you've added resources to a child's appendix (Chapter 13), they print at the back as tidy photo-cards — picture, name and your description. No prices and no shop links appear in the report — that information is for your eyes in the Library, not for the family's copy. The appendix is purely a helpful, clean handout of the recommended items.

How to export a report

  1. Open the client's record and go to the Export tab (or tap Export report from the Assessment tab).
  2. Glance at the Preview on the left to check it reads the way you want. Remember: anything blank is already left out for you.
  3. Decide whether you want your logo on this copy using the Show logo toggle (on for a polished letterhead, off for a plain document).
  4. Click Export PDF to get the standard file to email or print, Export Word if you'd like to make a few manual edits in Word first, or Print / Preview to send it straight to a printer.
  5. The file downloads to your computer's Downloads folder, named with the client and date, ready to attach to an email.
Because Preview, PDF and Word all come from the same engine, you don't need to “check the PDF matches the Word” — they always do.

Quick Exports

You don't always need the full report. The Quick Exports give you faster outputs:

  • Client One-Pager — a single-page summary of the person (key details and headline findings), available as a PDF or Word file. Handy for a school, a GP or a quick referral.
  • Full report — the complete assessment report, as above.

More than one assessment

If a child has had several dated assessments (an Initial and then an Annual Review, say), each one has its own export. Open the assessment you want from the Overview and export it — earlier reports stay exactly as they were, so last year's report never changes when you write this year's.

8 · Intake forms & consent

Send a parent or client a secure online form, and their answers come straight back to you — encrypted, so only you can read them. From a returned form you can create the client and file their consent in a couple of clicks.

One-time setup

Before anyone can send you a form, you need to switch intake on — just once.

  1. Open the Intake tab.
  2. Click Set up secure intake. That's the whole job — your forms can now receive submissions.
If you skip this, anyone trying to fill in your form sees a message saying the form is “not set up to receive submissions.” If a parent ever reports that, come here and run Set up secure intake once.

The two sets of forms

There are two ready-made bundles depending on who you're seeing:

  • Child set — a Consent form and a Parent Questionnaire (the parent fills these in about their child).
  • Adult set — a Consent form and an Adult Questionnaire (the client fills these in about themselves).

How the intake flow works

Why it's private: the answers are scrambled (encrypted) in the sender's own browser before they travel anywhere, using your key. That means nobody in between — not the internet, not any server — can read them. Only your unlocked dashboard can open them.

How to send forms to a client

  1. Go to the Intake tab.
  2. Choose the child set or the adult set depending on who you're seeing.
  3. Either email the secure links straight from here (the intake-forms email template fills in automatically — see Chapter 15), or use Copy link to paste a link into your own email or a text message.
  4. Send the Consent link and the Questionnaire link to the parent or client. They open the link, fill it in on any device, and submit — no account or password needed at their end.
No internet at the appointment, or a parent who'd rather do it on paper? Use the offline option: download a blank Word or PDF of either form, fill it in together, and upload the completed copy to the client's Documents. You get the same record either way.

How to turn a submission into a client and filed consent

When a form comes back, it waits in your Intake inbox. What you do next depends on which kind it is.

A returned Questionnaire → Create client

  1. Open the Intake tab and click the new Questionnaire submission to read it.
  2. Click Create client. The dashboard builds a brand-new client record and pours the questionnaire answers straight into it — name, date of birth, history and so on — ready for you to check.
  3. Save. You now have a full client record without re-typing anything the parent already wrote.

A returned Consent → File consent

  1. Open the Consent submission from the Intake inbox.
  2. Click File consent. You'll be asked which client it belongs to — the matching client is auto-suggested, so usually you just confirm. (If the client doesn't exist yet, choose + New client to create them first.)
  3. Confirm. The dashboard saves a dated “Record of Consent” PDF into that client's Documents as your proof, and ticks “Consent form signed” on their checklist automatically.
Filing consent this way means you always have a tamper-proof, dated PDF on file — exactly what you'd want to show if anyone ever asked whether you had permission to assess.

9 · Calendar, sessions & notes

Book your appointments, see your week at a glance, and write up each session in a structured note — with the ✨ assistant on every field.

The Calendar

Open Calendar from the sidebar to see your appointments. You can switch between a weekly and a monthly view, and click any day to see what's on.

How to book a session

  1. In Calendar, click the day (and time) you want, or click + New session.
  2. Pick the client, then set the type (e.g. assessment, treatment, review), the date and time, the duration and the location.
  3. Save. The session appears on your calendar and in that client's Sessions tab.
Keep your phone diary in step. On any session you can choose Open in Google Calendar to drop a copy into your Google diary (this is one-way — it sends the appointment out; changes you later make in Google don't flow back). To move everything into another calendar app at once, use Export all as .ics and import that file into Apple Calendar, Outlook or similar.

The Sessions list

The Sessions tab is the list of every session across all clients. Use the filters to narrow it down — by client, by type, by date range, or to show only sessions still needing a written note — so you can quickly find the one you want to write up.

How to log a past or unbooked session

Saw someone without booking it in first? You don't need to fake a calendar entry.

  1. In the Sessions tab (or the client's record), click Log a Session.
  2. Set the date, time, type and duration of the session that already happened.
  3. It opens straight into the note editor so you can write it up there and then.

How to write up a session note

Each note starts from your Session Note template (you can shape that template in Chapter 15), so every write-up has the same tidy structure — you're never staring at a blank page.

  1. Open the session (from the Calendar, the Sessions list, or the client's Sessions tab) and click into the note.
  2. Work down the pre-set headings, typing what happened in each. Every field can be drafted or tidied with its ✨ — and that includes the free-text Additional Notes box at the bottom.
  3. When you're done, decide whether to include your logo using the checkbox beside the Export buttons, then Export the note as a PDF or Word file if you need to share it.
Like everything else, session notes save themselves as you type — there's no Save button, just the Saving / Saved note in the top bar.

10 · The communication log

A simple, searchable record of every call, email and letter — who you spoke to, when, what was said, and any paperwork that went with it.

What the Comms log is for

Good practice means being able to say exactly when you spoke to a school, what a GP told you, or which letter you sent and when. The Comms log keeps all of that in one place. You can work from the main Comms tab in the sidebar (which shows contacts across every client) or from the Comms tab inside a single client's record (which shows just that person).

How to log a contact and attach a document

  1. Open Comms (from the sidebar, or inside the client's record) and click + Log contact.
  2. If you're on the main Comms tab, pick the client it relates to.
  3. Choose the type — call, email or letter — and the direction (incoming or outgoing), so it's clear who contacted whom.
  4. Write a short summary of what was discussed or sent. This is the bit you'll thank yourself for later.
  5. If there's paperwork — a letter you sent, a report a school emailed you — use Attach a document to add it. The file is saved into that client's Documents and linked to this log entry, so you can open it straight from the contact record. (This works from the main Comms tab too, not just inside a client.)
  6. Save. The entry appears in the client's communication history in date order.
Follow-ups don't get forgotten. If a contact needs chasing, set a follow-up flag on it. Flagged follow-ups surface on your Home dashboard and in the 🔔 bell, so an unanswered email or a callback you promised won't slip through the cracks.
Because an attached document lands in Documents and links both ways, you'll always find it whether you go looking in the Comms log or in the client's files.

11 · Documents

Every file you keep for a client — referrals, reports, consent forms, school paperwork, photos — tidy, tagged and easy to find.

How Documents are organised

Open the Documents tab to see your files grouped by client, so each person's paperwork sits together. At the top of the screen a storage meter shows how much room you're using, so you always know where you stand.

Every file carries a category, chosen from a dropdown on the file itself. The category tiles along the top let you filter the view to just one kind of document at a time — tap “Consent & Referral”, for instance, and you see only those. The categories are:

CategoryWhat goes here
CorrespondenceLetters and emails to and from schools, GPs, families.
Consent & ReferralSigned consent forms (including the Record of Consent PDFs from intake) and referral letters.
Reports & AssessmentsYour finished reports and any assessments done elsewhere.
School & EHCPSchool reports, IEPs, EHCP paperwork and panel documents.
Screenshots & PhotosPhotos from sessions, handwriting samples, equipment pictures.
OtherAnything that doesn't fit the above.

How to upload and categorise a document

  1. Open the Documents tab, or the Documents tab inside a client's record.
  2. Click Upload and choose the file (or files) from your computer.
  3. If you're on the main Documents tab, pick which client the file belongs to.
  4. Set the category using the dropdown on the file, so it lands under the right tile.
  5. Done — it saves automatically and appears under that client, filed by category.
Some tagging happens for you. Files that arrive through other parts of the app are categorised automatically — a consent PDF filed from intake is tagged Consent & Referral, and a photo you add during a session is tagged Screenshots & Photos. You can always change a category later from its dropdown.

12 · Finances: invoices, mileage, expenses & tax

The whole money side of your practice in one place — raise an invoice in under a minute, log your travel and expenses as you go, and have everything totalled and ready when the tax year ends. This is a big chapter because it does a lot; take it section by section.

The Finances tab is divided into sub-tabs — Invoices, Mileage, Expenses, Income and Tax — plus an Annual Summary. You'll spend most of your time in Invoices; the rest quietly build up the totals that make your tax return painless.

Before you start, fill in Settings → Bank Details and your Services & prices (Chapter 2). Once those are set, most of an invoice fills itself in.

Invoices

An invoice is the bill you send a client. The dashboard builds it from your saved prices, adds up the totals, tracks whether it's been paid, and exports it as a tidy document.

How to create an invoice

  1. Go to Finances → Invoices and click New invoice.
  2. Pick the client it's for. Their details fill in at the top.
  3. Add your line items. As you start typing, they auto-fill from your Services & prices list — choose “Motor co-ordination assessment” and the £480 drops in; choose “Report writing” and set the hours, and it works out the amount. You can still type over any figure for a one-off.
  4. Add a Travel line if you're charging for the journey. You have three ways to price it:
    • Quoted — a flat, agreed figure that you simply type in. This is the default.
    • Calculated — the dashboard works it out as journey minutes × your travel rate (so 40 minutes at £25/hr = £16.67). You can still override the result if you'd like to round it.
  5. Set the invoice date and the due date (when you'd like to be paid by).
  6. Save. The invoice gets the next number automatically and lands in your Invoices list marked Unpaid.

The fields on an invoice

FieldWhat it does
ClientWho the invoice is billed to; pulls their name and details in.
Invoice numberAssigned automatically, in sequence — you don't type it.
Invoice dateThe date the invoice is raised.
Due dateWhen payment is expected; drives overdue reminders.
Line itemsWhat you're charging for; descriptions and rates auto-fill from Services & prices.
Travel lineQuoted (flat, the default) or Calculated (minutes × travel rate, overridable).
TotalAdded up for you from the line items.
Bank detailsPulled from Settings so the client knows where to pay.
StatusPaid or Unpaid — you toggle it when the money arrives.

How to mark an invoice paid

  1. Open the invoice (or find it in the Invoices list) and click Mark paid when the money comes in.
  2. The status flips to Paid and it drops out of your unpaid total.
  3. Use the “Next unpaid →” nudge to jump straight to the next invoice still owing, so you can work through your outstanding bills in one sitting.

How to export or email an invoice

  1. Open the invoice and choose Export PDF (the usual choice to send), Export Word (to edit first), or Export CSV (for your records or accountant).
  2. To send it, use Email invoice — the invoice email template fills in for you (Chapter 15); attach the PDF and send.
Payment status at a glance: the Invoices list shows what's paid, what's unpaid and what's overdue, and the Unpaid Invoices tile on Home keeps the number in front of you.

Mileage / Travel log

Separate from charging a client for travel, the Mileage sub-tab is your own record of business journeys — the one HMRC cares about.

How to log a journey

  1. Go to Finances → Mileage and click Add journey.
  2. Enter the date, where you travelled from and to, the miles, and the purpose (e.g. “home visit — A. Sample”).
  3. The log applies your per-mile rate (set in Services & prices, e.g. 45p/mile) and keeps a running total.
  4. When you need it, export the whole log as PDF, Word or CSV.

Expenses

The Expenses sub-tab records money you spend on the business — equipment, test forms, training, postage.

How to add an expense

  1. Go to Finances → Expenses and click Add expense.
  2. Enter the date, choose a category, and type the amount.

17 · Your data: safety, sync & backups

Your records are private by design and backed up in layers, so the everyday worries — “could someone read this?”, “what if my laptop dies?”, “what if a sync wipes my work?” — are quietly taken care of.

Everything is encrypted

All of your data is encrypted with your master password before it's ever saved or uploaded. In plain terms: your records are scrambled with a key only your password unlocks, so only you can read them. Anything stored on your device or in the cloud is held in that scrambled form — useless to anyone without your password.

Layers of backup

You're protected by several backups working together, so a single mishap can't lose your work:

  • Continuous local saves — as you type, your work is saved on your device. (That's the Saving / Saved you see in the top bar.)
  • Daily backup files — a dated backup is written so you can step back to an earlier copy if you ever need to.
  • Cloud Sync — an encrypted copy kept in the cloud, with a restorable history you can roll back to under Settings → Backups.
  • Optional daily OneDrive snapshot — if you turn it on, a daily snapshot is also tucked into your OneDrive for an extra off-device copy.
Your recovery key is essential. Encryption means even support cannot open your data for you. If you ever forget your master password, the recovery key (Chapter 2) is the only way back in. Please make sure you've saved it somewhere safe and separate.

Sync won't overwrite your work

When you log in, if the cloud is holding a different or older copy than the one on your device, the dashboard will not silently overwrite your device. It keeps your local data and asks you first, so a background sync can never quietly wipe records you've just written. You're always in control of which copy wins.

Moving to a new computer

Because your encrypted data lives in Cloud Sync, getting onto a new machine is simply a matter of opening the dashboard there and unlocking with the same master password — your records sync down. (This is also why the recovery key matters: same password, same data, anywhere.)

Erasing a client's data (GDPR)

If a discharged client asks you to erase their data, you can do it cleanly and provably.

  1. Open the client's record and choose the GDPR erasure option.
  2. The dashboard downloads a backup first, so you keep a copy for your own legal records before anything is removed.
  3. You'll be asked to type DELETE to confirm — a deliberate step so it can't happen by accident.
  4. Confirm, and that client's data is erased from the app.

18 · Annie, your assistant

A friendly helper you can ask, in plain English, “how do I…?” — right where you're working.

Where to find her

Annie is the floating ✨ button in the bottom-right corner of every screen. Tap it any time and ask your question the way you'd ask a colleague — “how do I mark an invoice as paid?”, “where do I file a consent form?”, “how do I switch a child to the Marie report?” — and she'll talk you through it.

How she works

Annie runs on Claude (from Anthropic), powered by an Anthropic API key stored only on your device. That single key powers everything clever in the app: Annie's chat and all the ✨ Draft / Refine writing buttons throughout the dashboard. Add the key once (Chapter 2, or when Annie first prompts you) and it all comes to life.

What Annie can and can't see. Annie helps you use the app — she explains how to do things and points you to where things live. She cannot see your data or your figures: she won't read a client's notes or tell you a total. Ask “where do I find unpaid invoices?” and she'll send you to the right place; she won't tell you the amount.

19 · Troubleshooting & FAQ

Quick, practical answers to the things most likely to come up. If something's not behaving, start here.

I've forgotten my master password — what now?

Use your recovery key to get back in (Chapter 2). Without it, there's no way to recover the data — the encryption that keeps your records private also means nobody, including support, can unlock them for you. If you haven't set a recovery key yet and you can still get in, do it today: Settings → Security → Generate recovery key.

Someone filling in my form saw “not set up to receive submissions.”

Your intake just needs switching on. Open the Intake tab and click Set up secure intake — once only (Chapter 8). After that, your forms accept submissions and the message won't appear again.

My intake submissions won't load, or I see a “401”.

That points to Cloud Sync not being connected. Open Settings → Cloud Sync and click Connect & Test until you see the green “Cloud synced” confirmation, then try the submissions again.

Where did my attached document go?

Straight into the Documents tab, filed under that client and tagged with a category. Anything you attach to a comms entry is saved there and linked both ways, so you can open it from the Comms log or from Documents (Chapters 10–11).

A section is missing from my report.

Two friendly reasons, both by design. Either that section's “In report / Not needed” toggle is set to Not needed — switch it back to In report (Chapters 5 and 7). Or the section was left blank: empty fields and sub-headings are intentionally omitted so your report never shows empty headings. Fill it in and it appears.

How do I get my data onto a new computer?

Just open the dashboard on the new machine and unlock with the same master password. Your encrypted records sync down via Cloud Sync — nothing to copy across by hand (Chapter 17).

Is my data private?

Yes. Everything is encrypted with your master password before it's saved or uploaded, so it's only ever stored as a scrambled copy that only your password can open. Even the cloud backup can't be read without it.

The AI button (✨) isn't doing anything.

It needs an Anthropic API key, stored on your device. Add it through Annie (the ✨ button, bottom-right) or under Settings. One key powers Annie and every ✨ Draft/Refine button (Chapters 2 and 18).

There's an orange “TEST VERSION” banner — is something wrong?

Not at all — that's the safe practice copy with sample clients, for trying things out. Your real, private records are in the normal version with no banner (Chapter 1).

I can't find a Save button.

There isn't one, and you don't need it. Everything saves itself as you type — watch for Saving / Saved in the top bar. You can close the tab whenever you like and your work will be there next time.
